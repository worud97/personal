Kindness Online

Timeline: October 2018 - Current

Tools: Qualitative / Research / Interviews / Surveys

Have you ever found yourself embroiled in an argument on social media? We are studying the ways in which the design of a platform changes the tenor of a conversation and what leads to friendship, fights, and reconciliation.

Challenge

Conversations online can be directed by many different factors: anonymity, disconnection, and even the design of a platform. These factors can lead people to have destructive conversations in which arguments occur but are not resolved well. We are interested in how specifically the design of a platform, with other factors, can improve conversations online to be more constructive rather than destructive.

Approaches

In order to solve this challenge, our approach is to research and design prototypes to guide more constructive conversations online. Our current plan is to publish a research paper with all our findings by September 2019.

Research

Research / Literature Review

Prior to doing anything else, we first conducted literature reviews to understand what arguments and forgiveness are, and how online relationships and conversations are constructed.

Interviews

We conducted 23 interviews, both in-person and over phone. Through the interviews we gained knowledge on how arguments arise online, how they end in different circumstances and received inupt for possible design ideas.

Surveys

In order to gather demographic information, we distributed surveys to our interviewees after the interview.

Ideation / Sketches

On realtimeboard, we aggregated and categorized data from interview transcripts.

These are low fidelity prototypes for three different categories: word count, time sending, and time reading.
