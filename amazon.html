Simple Recycle

Timeline: November 2017

Tools: Online Survey / Research

Our project’s goal is to create an innovative, yet an easy design that will help people divert trash within 0.5 seconds with matching system in order to help the University of Washington reach its goal of diverting 70% of the university's waste from landfill by 2020.

More about Amazon Catalyst

Problem

Current recycling and composting systems are not fully adequate enough to successfully assist people in diverting trash because it is confusing and time-consuming. Current systems focus on the categorization of waste bins rather than the trash itself.

Approaches

Since current systems focus on solely categorizing the trash bins without taking into consideration of people’s knowledge on the composition of the actual trash itself, we wanted to focus on designing and specifically classifying the trash to pair trash with a trash can.

Through research and survey, our team concluded that it is best to make a universal symbol with clear color and shape distinctions for landfill, recycle, and compost that is boundless to any disabilities, language barriers, and any forms of cultural differences.

Research

Figure. Current recycling system at the University of Washington.

We first carried out research to understand 1) how destructive trash is to our environment, 2) how people perceive and understand color, 3) why current systems are unsuccessful in diverting all trash.

Then we created and distributed an online survey to learn about people’s opinions on recycling. According to this survey, 97.8% stated that recycling was important. However, the majority, 85.7% confessed that recycling was confusing due to uncertainty about the composition of the material of trash.
